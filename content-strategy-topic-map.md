# EilatJobs.com Content Strategy — Topic Map

## Business Context
- **Site**: eilatjobs.com (Barak Services / ברק שירותים)
- **Position**: #1 for "עבודה באילת" — exact-match domain advantage
- **Audience**: Discharged soldiers (18-22), young adults, 40+ career switchers relocating to Eilat
- **Goal**: Capture long-tail search traffic, build topical authority beyond "jobs", generate qualified leads
- **Competitor gap**: Maof owns "career" and "beyond seasonality" content; nobody owns salary benchmarks or relocation guides

---

## Content Pillars (4)

```
eilatjobs.com/blog
├── Pillar 1: עבודה באילת (Jobs in Eilat) ← existing strength, deepen
├── Pillar 2: קריירה באילת (Career Growth) ← attack Maof's territory
├── Pillar 3: מעבר לאילת (Relocation Guide) ← untapped, high intent
└── Pillar 4: שכר ותנאים (Salary & Benefits) ← zero competition
```

### Cross-linking Strategy
- Every **Jobs** article (Pillar 1) links to the **Salary** spoke for that field (Pillar 4)
- Every **Salary** article (Pillar 4) links to the **Career Growth** spoke for that field (Pillar 2)
- Every **Career** article (Pillar 2) links to the **Relocation** hub (Pillar 3)
- The **Relocation** hub (Pillar 3) links to **Quick Apply** (`/outreach`) as the primary CTA
- All articles link to the Quick Apply form as the conversion endpoint

## Key Insight

**EilatJobs currently competes on transactional keywords only** ("דרושים באילת", "עבודה באילת"). By expanding into informational content (salary, relocation, career growth), the site captures candidates **earlier in their decision journey** — before they even decide to move to Eilat. This is exactly how Maof is stealing long-tail traffic with their deep blog articles.

The salary benchmarks pillar is the biggest opportunity: **nobody in the entire Israeli job market publishes Eilat-specific salary data**. Owning this space means every candidate researching Eilat pay will land on eilatjobs.com.
